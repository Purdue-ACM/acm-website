A High School Programming Competition

Co-hosted by Purdue's ACM chapter and ExactTarget

Challenging problems

No specific algorithm/data structure knowledge needed, just bring good problem solving skills and your creativity.

Cool prizes

Compete for laptops, tablets, and ExactTarget swag.

At one of America's best universities

Talk with counselors and admissions representatives to learn about Purdue's CS curriculum and application requirements. Explore campus and talk with current students. Discover what it's like to be a Boilermaker!

We're inviting high school students around the midwest to compete in a 4-hour programming competition.

On November 1st, teams will get the chance to travel to Purdue and solve 15 challenging programming problems, interact with current CS students, and tour campus. The top 3 teams that solve the most problems will receive laptops, tablets, and gift cards, but everyone has the chance to meet with our sponsor, ExactTarget, and pickup tshirts and swag. Our application will be available on September 15th. Any student interested in programming/technology is welcome, regardless of skill level! Lunch from Panda Express will be provided.

FAQs

When and where exactly will it be taking place?
It will take place in the Lawson Computer Science building on Purdue's campus at 305 N. University St in West Lafayette, IN on November 1st, 2014, from 11am-5pm.

Can you tell me more about the competition?
Accepted students will form teams of 1-2 people. They will be assigned 1 computer to work on in a lab and Internet access will be restricted (using the Internet on your smart phone is not allowed, either). We will provide access to official documentation for each programming language and their respective standard libraries. Each team will receive a packet of 15 programming problems of varying difficulty and will be given 4 hours to solve them. The team with the highest amount of problems solved will be declared the winner.

We'll have a practice session first to get everyone acquainted with the judge system and provide a free lunch from Panda Express afterwards.

What prizes will be awarded?
Prizes include laptops, tablets and $100 gift cards.

Who's allowed to apply?
Any student, ages 13-18 who attend a high school or equivalent and are interested in pursing a technology-related degree in college are welcome to apply. Everyone of all skill levels is encouraged to apply!

Are parents/teachers welcome too?
Absolutely! We will have Purdue counselors and admissions representatives there to answer any questions you may have about the computer science curriculum, Purdue application requirements, scholarships, or college life in general.

What languages can we use in the competition?
Java, C/C++, and Python

Is there any specific knowledge we need to be able to compete?
Not particularly. Just have a decent grasp on any of the languages above and have good problem solving skills. Specific knowledge about data structures and algorithms isn't needed, though it can't hurt!

What will we be working with?
Each team will work on a desktop lab computer in the Lawson CS building. Use of any other personal devices including laptops and phones are prohibited. Internet access will be restricted except for official documentation of Java, Python, and C/C++ standard libraries.
